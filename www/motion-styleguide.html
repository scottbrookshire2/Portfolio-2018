Creating our first motion system

Our primary user need was centered around helping people find fun things to do. We reshaped our event discovery experience across our ecosystem to bring more life, levity and fun to the app. To become more of a truset source we elevated the emphasis on city and integreated editorial and third party content. Relating the brand tenants to animation principles

We wanted to create a new consumer-centric visual system that balances the Eventbrite brand with the excitment of discovering something fun to do.

Radically Simple

Motion and animation are disciplined in their use and execution. They serve to accentuate the user experience — not distract from it. Animation should be purposefully included, not gratuitously showcased.

Inviting

Motion and animation should draw the user into the brand experience. They should create a connection between the viewer and the content, sparking an emotional response and inviting interaction.

Trustworthy

Motion and animation should be predictable, using natural, predictive movements that ground the viewer. Our animation explains what’s happening by connecting an action with a logical consequence. In doing so, it makes the interaction more trustworthy by reinforcing the user’s actions and building confidence.

Dynamic

Motion and animation transform an ordinary interaction into a meaningful and memorable one. They create moments of delight by introducing unexpected experiences. Our motion isn’t sterile or mechanical, it brings life into the interface and deepens the user’s connection with our content.
