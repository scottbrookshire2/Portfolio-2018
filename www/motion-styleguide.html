Motion Styleguide

Creating our first motion system

We wanted to create a system for motion design at Eventbrite, but were never able to dedicate resources to do so. As a side project, a few designers got together and created our first motion design sytlyeguide. Inspired by atomic design and with the goal of being thoughtfully incorporated into the Eventbrite Design System, we started from the bottom and worked our way up. I helped project manage the effort, shape our current brand tenants around motion principles, animate abstract concepts into a concrete ideas, develop the first rev of tokens (centered around easing, timing and delay) and created and facilitated After Effects workshops for the design org. Shout out to Dave O' Brien and Sun Dai for collaborating on the effort.

The Challenge

There was no precedent for a motion system at Eventbrite, and to make this system feel like us we had to understand who we were. Our first step was to relate our brand tenants to animation principles.

Radically Simple

Motion and animation are disciplined in their use and execution. They serve to accentuate the user experience — not distract from it. Animation should be purposefully included, not gratuitously showcased.

Inviting

Motion and animation should draw the user into the brand experience. They should create a connection between the viewer and the content, sparking an emotional response and inviting interaction.

Trustworthy

Motion and animation should be predictable, using natural, predictive movements that ground the viewer. Our animation explains what’s happening by connecting an action with a logical consequence. In doing so, it makes the interaction more trustworthy by reinforcing the user’s actions and building confidence.

Dynamic

Motion and animation transform an ordinary interaction into a meaningful and memorable one. They create moments of delight by introducing unexpected experiences. Our motion isn’t sterile or mechanical, it brings life into the interface and deepens the user’s connection with our content.

Building the motion framework from scratch

To unblock the effort I developed tokens around timing, easing and dealy. We used these tokens to create atoms such as fade out, and bounce.
